The Quiet Power of Funke

Published on: June 15, 2023 Author: Women's Empowerment Series

"Does anyone have questions?" The speaker's voice jolted Funke back to the training room. She had questions, but she'd rather freeze to death at the North Pole than stand to speak.

Funke rarely spoke up in meetings. She was shy, restrained, quiet and thoughtful, and she believed her quiet voice didn't carry weight in a world that rewarded loudness.

She admired her brother's wife, Eunice, the VP of a fintech company in Lagos who spoke to rooms with confidence and charm. No fear. Just confidence. Funke wanted that: to speak up without that lump of anxiety in her throat.

Funke noticed how people shivered around loud and aggressive personalities. She knew she could never be that, but she wanted to lead without coercion to inspire rather than intimidate. So she started practicing quiet, calibrated assertiveness: she slowed her speech, stopped over-explaining, and trusted pauses as much as words.

The result wasn't theatrical. There was no cinematic transformation but people began to listen. Respect arrived, subtly and then suddenly. You don't have to shout to be powerful. Calm is not weakness; it's quiet strength.
