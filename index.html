The Quiet Power of Funke

“Does anyone have questions?” The speaker’s voice jolted Funke back to the training room. She had questions but she would rather freeze to death at the North Pole than stand to speak.

Funke rarely spoke up in meetings. She was shy, restrained, quiet, and thoughtful, and she believed her quiet voice didn’t carry weight in a world that rewarded loudness.

She admired her brother’s wife, Eunice, the VP of a Fintech Company in Lagos. Eunice spoke to audiences with confidence and charm. No fear. Just confidence. Funke wanted to be that. She wanted to speak up without fear.

Funke also saw how people shivered and shrunk around loud and aggressive leaders. She knew she could never be that, but she desired to lead calmly without making people afraid. She began practicing calm assertiveness. She stopped stuttering, over-explaining, and speaking too fast. People began to listen.

What happened? Hell did not freeze over. Instead, she started gaining respect — instantly. She became confident and gained admiration. You don’t have to shout to be powerful. Calm is not weakness, but quiet strength. Join our community of women rewriting the rules.
